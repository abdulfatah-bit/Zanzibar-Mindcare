About

Abdulfatah Khamis

Abdulfatah Khamis is a Psychologist and Researcher with over five years of experience at the intersection of mental health, public health, and community development. His work spans clinical practice, academic research, programme management, and university-level teaching, with a strong commitment to making mental health support more accessible, evidence-based, and culturally responsive.

Currently based in Türkiye as a recipient of the Turkish Government Scholarship (YTB), he is pursuing a PhD in Psychological Counseling at Muğla Sıtkı Koçman University (MSKÜ) and has been selected for Erasmus+ outgoing mobility at PH Schwäbisch Gmünd, Germany (2026–2027).

In his clinical role at the Zanzibar Ministry of Health, Abdulfatah has provided psychotherapy to more than 400 clients and led the establishment of a multidisciplinary centre at Jitima District Hospital — serving over 350,000 individuals — providing integrated psychological, medical, and social support for survivors of abuse and gender-based violence, leading a team of more than 20 professionals.

He is a graduate of the YALI East Africa Regional Leadership Program and a member of the American Psychological Association (APA), ISPCAN, TPA, and ZAPCA. He remains committed to advancing accessible, culturally responsive, and community-centred mental health services across Africa and beyond.
